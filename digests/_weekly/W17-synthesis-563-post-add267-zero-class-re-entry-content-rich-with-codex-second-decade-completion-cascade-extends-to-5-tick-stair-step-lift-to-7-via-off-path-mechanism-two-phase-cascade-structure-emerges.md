## The 5-tick cascade's structural reframing

Synth #562 introduced the 4-tick cascade as a structural unit. With Add.267 the cascade extends to 5 ticks but its semantics shift:

**Ticks 1-3 (Add.263/264/265)**: Pure cluster-instantiation phase. Each tick adds axis content via per-tick regime events (axis counts 5/5/6).
**Tick 4 (Add.266)**: Cluster-instantiation phase continues with cross-actor handoff; axis count holds at 6.
**Tick 5 (Add.267)**: Cluster-instantiation phase **continues without merge activity**. Axis count rises to 7 via pause-spectrum events (codex decade-completion, PJL expansion, mid-gap-empty sextet) and anchor-state events (null re-instantiation). Carrier-bound propagation **suspends** (no opencode merge) but cluster-cardinality **continues to grow**.

This 5-tick cascade is therefore **two-phase**: a merge-driven cluster-instantiation phase (ticks 1-4) and a pause-driven cluster-instantiation phase (tick 5). The transition between phases is a **carrier-state flip** (active→silent) rather than a cascade termination.

Two-phase cascades have implications for cascade-termination modeling: synth #562's three carrier-bound termination criteria (carrier-silence ≥1 tick, zero-class re-entry within carrier, cross-carrier propagation) now need refinement. Carrier-silence at a single tick does NOT terminate a cascade if the cluster-cardinality continues to grow via pause-spectrum events. **Carrier-silence with cluster-cardinality contraction** is the actual termination signal — both must occur together for cascade-termination.

The refined termination criterion: **a cascade terminates when EITHER (i) carrier-silence ≥2 consecutive ticks OR (ii) carrier-silence ≥1 tick AND cluster-cardinality ≤ 4**. Add.267 satisfies neither — single-tick carrier-silence with cluster-cardinality 7 → cascade is in **probationary phase**, not terminated.

## Risk lens

- **Two-phase cascade interpretation risk**: the merge-driven vs pause-driven phase distinction is observed at N=1 cascade (this one). Generalizing to "all W17 cascades exhibit two-phase structure" requires multiple cascade instances. Treat the two-phase claim as cascade-specific until N ≥ 3 cascades.
- **Decade-completion attractor risk**: the decade-completion-as-rebound-attractor hypothesis is at N=0 confirmations (codex n=20 just instantiated; rebound at n=21 vs not-yet-observed). Only multiple decade-completion events with subsequent rebound observations can confirm or falsify. Until then, treat the discrete-attractor hypothesis as exploratory.
- **Aggregate-axis-count decoupling risk**: the claim that cascade axis-count can grow via non-propagation-vector mechanisms is structurally significant but its scope is unclear. Does it apply to all axis types (carrier-state, anchor-state, joint composite, transition axis) equally? Or only to pause-spectrum and aggregate axes? The current data is insufficient to partition axis-count growth by mechanism.
- **Refined termination criterion risk**: the refined criterion (carrier-silence ≥2 ticks OR carrier-silence ≥1 tick AND cluster-cardinality ≤4) is a post-hoc refinement after Add.267 falsified the synth #562 criteria. Post-hoc refinement is methodologically vulnerable to overfitting on N=1. The criterion should be tested on the next cascade in W17 (whenever it occurs) before accepting as a generalized model.
- **Bimodal-balanced anchor distribution interpretation risk**: the null≡fresh exact-balance at 11-tick window is a single observation. Whether bimodal balance is a structural attractor or an arbitrary alignment depends on subsequent ticks. If Add.268 breaks the balance toward either side and subsequent ticks oscillate around the boundary, bimodal balance is a structural attractor. If Add.268 breaks the balance and subsequent ticks drift monotonically away, bimodal balance is an arbitrary alignment that happened to occur once.
