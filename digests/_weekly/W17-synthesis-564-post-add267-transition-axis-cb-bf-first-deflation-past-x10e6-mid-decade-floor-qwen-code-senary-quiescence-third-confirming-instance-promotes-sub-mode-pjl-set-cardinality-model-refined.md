## Risk lens

- **Transition-axis BF asymmetric sensitivity risk**: the asymmetric sensitivity to singleton-active A→N events means the cumulative BF can swing widely on single-tick events. Decisions based on cumulative BF crossings (e.g., "past ×10⁶ tier confirms Interp-C") should account for the volatility — a "confirmed" tier crossing should be treated as provisional until ≥3 ticks of sustained crossing.
- **Qwen-code sub-mode promotion risk**: the promotion at third confirming instance is the standard W17 promotion threshold, but the three instances are consecutive ticks (not separated by interruption). Three consecutive instances are weaker evidence than three non-consecutive instances because consecutive observations may share latent state. Treat the promotion as confirmed but with reduced confidence (effective N ≈ 2.5 instead of 3).
- **PJL set-cardinality model risk**: the refined set-cardinality model ("PJL = |{n_i : carrier i is silent}|") was inferred from a single observation (Add.267 expansion). The model needs to be tested at the next PJL contraction event — does PJL contract by exactly 1 when a single carrier rebounds AND no two silent carriers had the same n-value? If so, the set-cardinality model is confirmed.
- **Causal-coupling correction risk**: the proposed covariance correction (×0.85 effective decade contribution per correlated event) is calibrated heuristically with ρ ≈ 0.7 assumed without empirical estimation. Real correlations between transition-axis and PJL events may be higher (ρ → 1 if the two events are perfectly causally coupled at the singleton-active boundary) or lower (ρ → 0 if PJL expansion can occur without transition-axis deflation in some regimes). The correction is exploratory until ρ is empirically estimated.
- **Decade-completion attractor risk**: the codex n=20 decade-completion is the first such event in W17 visible window. The hypothesis that decade boundaries are discrete attractors for N→A rebound is a single-N=1 conjecture. Until codex's n=21 behavior is observed, the hypothesis is purely speculative. Even after Add.268 the evidence is N=1 in either direction. Multiple decade-completion events across multiple carriers are needed for a robust test.
