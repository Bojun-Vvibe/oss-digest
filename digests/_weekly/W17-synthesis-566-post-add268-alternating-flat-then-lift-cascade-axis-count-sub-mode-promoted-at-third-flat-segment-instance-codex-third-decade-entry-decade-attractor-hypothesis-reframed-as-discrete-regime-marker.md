## What's new at the codex third-decade-entry event

Codex entered its third decade of consecutive silence at Add.268 (n=21). This is the **first cataloged third-decade-entry event** for any single monitored carrier in the W17 visible window. The decade boundaries are:

- 1st decade: n in [1, 10] — interior-decade default behavior.
- 2nd decade: n in [11, 20] — long-tail post-decade-boundary regime (synth #550 era documented for litellm).
- 3rd decade: n in [21, 30] — extreme long-tail, no prior cataloged carriers in this regime.

The codex sequence n=11 → n=20 spanned ticks Add.258-267 (10 ticks). The n=21 entry at Add.268 places codex at the leading edge of the third-decade region, with no prior cataloged carrier-rebound data in the [21, 30] range.

**Decade-attractor hypothesis** (introduced at synth #564 as a single-N=1 conjecture): decade boundaries are discrete attractors for N→A rebound. The hypothesis predicts that carriers are more likely to rebound at decade boundaries (n in {10, 20, 30, ...}) than at interior-decade points. The Add.267 codex n=20 instance was the first observation point; the n=21 sustain (i.e., codex did NOT rebound at the n=20 boundary) is the **first observation against the hypothesis**.

But the hypothesis can be reframed: decade boundaries are attractors for **rebound-or-decade-completion-to-next-tier**. Under this reframing, the n=20 → n=21 transition is itself a structural event (the carrier completes a decade and immediately enters the next one). The n=20 → n=21 transition is a **decade-completion-then-decade-entry doublet** at minimum residence — first cataloged in W17 visible window.

## Pause-spectrum decade-attractor hypothesis: empirical signature progression

Across the synth #564 → synth #566 progression, the decade-attractor hypothesis has acquired its first empirical signatures:

- Synth #550: documented litellm post-decade-boundary rebound failures at n in [11, 17] — initial evidence of long-tail behavior past the n=10 boundary, but framed as continuous mode-tail rather than discrete attractor.
- Synth #564: codex completed n=20 (synth #564 anchor); proposed decade-completion as a discrete pause-spectrum event. Single-N=1 conjecture.
- Synth #566 (this synth): codex extends to n=21, instantiating decade-completion-then-decade-entry doublet. Now N=2 doublet with a structural connection.

Open questions on the decade-attractor hypothesis:

(Q1) Are decade boundaries genuinely attractors for rebound (carriers tend to rebound at n=10, 20, 30, ...) OR are they merely **transition markers** between regimes with no special rebound probability?

(Q2) If decade boundaries are attractors for rebound, why did codex sustain past n=20? Possible answers: (a) decade-attractor effect is weak relative to the carrier-specific silence-momentum; (b) decade attractors apply only at the first decade boundary (n=10) and weaken at subsequent boundaries; (c) decade-attractor effect requires a sufficiently active carrier-state-of-the-world to trigger (e.g., other carriers active); (d) the hypothesis is wrong and decade boundaries have no special status.

(Q3) Even if decade boundaries are not rebound attractors, the codex n=20 → n=21 transition has a discrete-step character (the completion of one decade and the beginning of another) that may be **structurally informative** independent of rebound probability. The empirical signature of this discrete-step character is observable but its causal mechanism is unspecified.

If no, the co-occurrence at Add.268 is coincidental and the two events should be analyzed as independent.

A first-pass test: count the number of W17 visible-window ticks where (cascade axis-count event) AND (pause-spectrum event) both occurred within the same tick. If the count is significantly higher than expected under independence, the periodic-cycling hypothesis gains support. The Add.268 event is the **first such joint occurrence** in the visible window — N=1 is too small for any inference, but the marker is established.

## Risk lens

- **Alternating-flat-then-lift sub-mode promotion-at-axis-ceiling risk**: the sub-mode is promoted at axis-count level 7, just one step below the theoretical ceiling at level 8. If Add.269 lifts to 8, the sub-mode reaches the ceiling and further empirical extension requires axis-framework expansion. The promotion is therefore **promotion-at-ceiling**, which means the sub-mode's predictive value is strongest in the few remaining ticks before ceiling-test. After ceiling-reach, the sub-mode either resolves (cascade contracts and we observe re-cascade dynamics) or requires re-framing (cascade extends past 8 axes which means the framework expanded).
- **Codex third-decade-entry as anomaly risk**: the third-decade entry is a single-N=1 event. Without comparable third-decade trajectories from other carriers, it's impossible to distinguish "codex is a typical third-decade carrier" from "codex is anomalously persistent". Watch for whether litellm (currently n=18) reaches n=20 and whether it then sustains into n=21 (cross-carrier confirmation) or rebounds at n=20 (codex was the anomaly).
- **Decade-attractor hypothesis reframing risk**: the synth #564 → synth #566 progression has reframed the hypothesis from "attractor" (rebound-biasing) to "discrete-marker" (regime-transitioning) without empirical support for either direction. Both reframings are post-hoc rationalizations of single-tick observations. The hypothesis may be entirely vacuous — decade boundaries may have no special status whatsoever, and the apparent "decade-completion" events are merely artifacts of decimal-counting on continuous silence trajectories. Test: re-bin silence trajectories at non-decimal cycle lengths (e.g., 7-tick bins, 13-tick bins) and check if equally-strong "boundary-completion" events emerge. If yes, the decade-attractor hypothesis is an artifact.
- **Periodic-cycling hypothesis premature speculation risk**: the joint-occurrence of cascade event and pause-spectrum event at Add.268 is N=1 — far too small to support a periodic-cycling hypothesis. The hypothesis is included as a "marker established" rather than a serious proposal. Avoid acting on the hypothesis until ≥3 joint-occurrence events accumulate.
- **Sub-mode promotion threshold consistency risk**: the W17 promotion threshold is "third confirming instance". For the alternating-flat-then-lift sub-mode, the three instances are flat-doublets at levels 5, 6, 7 — three different levels. For the qwen-code post-cycle quiescence sub-mode (synth #564), the three instances are sustains at n=4, n=5, n=6 — three different post-cycle silent durations. In both cases, the "three confirming instances" are at distinct conditions, not three repetitions of identical conditions. This suggests the W17 promotion threshold is **"three instances of the abstract pattern"**, not "three repetitions of the specific event". Worth noting because earlier synths sometimes treated three-of-the-same as the threshold.
- **Cumulative cascade-axis-count BF inflation risk**: with the sub-mode promoted, future flat-doublet observations at higher levels will be treated as confirming evidence for the sub-mode, contributing to amplification of the cumulative BF. But under bridge-tolerance (synth #565), cascades can extend across nulls, so cascade lengths may be artificially inflated. The combination (longer cascades + sub-mode-confirming axis-count growth) may double-count evidence in the cumulative BF. Watch for this when computing cumulative cascade-axis BF.
