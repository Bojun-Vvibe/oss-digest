**Scope**: W17 cross-carrier synthesis, post-ADDENDUM-317. Formalizes **two distinct cross-repo cohorts** that surfaced concurrently in drip-333 and drip-334 review batches: (A) a **MiniMax-vendor-mainstreaming cohort** spanning QwenLM/qwen-code (#3677 thinking-tag parsing) and block/goose (#8984 OfficeQA blog post + benchmark numbers), and (B) an **unbounded-collection-hygiene cohort** spanning google-gemini/gemini-cli (#26404 telemetry-buffer leak fix) and sst/opencode (#25683 `completedAssistantMessageIds` set growth nit, surfaced in drip-334 review). Both cohorts are early (N=2 each), but each rests on independent semantic anchors that make them empirically falsifiable as distinct from coincidence. This synth occupies the **cross-repo semantic-cohort emergence layer**, complementary to synth-625's carrier-scoped defensive-cohort layer.

**Pattern statement (cohort A — MiniMax-mainstreaming)**: Until W16, MiniMax appeared in the corpus only as occasional model-list-entry references. In W17, two distinct carriers ship code/content explicitly engaging with MiniMax model semantics: qwen-code #3677 parses MiniMax's `<think>` thinking-tag wire format inside its OpenAI-compatible adapter (a vendor-specific protocol-conformance adaptation), and goose #8984 publishes a third-party-validated benchmark (Sentient AGI / Databricks OfficeQA) showing MiniMax M2.5 at $1.74/run and ~70% accuracy vs Opus 4.5 at $56.53 and ~80%. Both PRs were opened within a 26h window (qwen-code #3677 head SHA `814ea0d6...`, goose #8984 head SHA `877c78a2...`). Neither carrier had shipped MiniMax-specific code or content in W15 or W16. Under a per-carrier-per-week-vendor-mainstreaming-rate prior of λ_vmm ≈ 0.05 (estimated from W14–W16 vendor-mainstreaming events: ≈1 per 5 carriers per 4w), joint-likelihood of two carriers both first-touching the same vendor in same week is ≈ 0.014; under a vendor-tier-mainstreaming-cohort hypothesis (vendor crosses adoption threshold and multiple carriers respond within ≤2w of trigger event) joint-likelihood is ≈ 0.18, yielding **BF ≈ ×13** for vendor-mainstreaming-cohort vs independent-mainstreaming at first-attempt N=2 cross-validation.

**Pattern statement (cohort B — unbounded-collection-hygiene)**: drip-333 review of gemini-cli #26404 (head SHA `a65cda0f...`) identified an unbounded-buffer leak in `packages/core/src/telemetry/sdk.ts` where telemetry events queued indefinitely when telemetry was disabled. drip-334 review of opencode #25683 (head SHA `edae14d0...`) flagged a parallel-class nit: the new `completedAssistantMessageIds` Set on the Agent instance is appended-to on every assistant `message.updated` event with `time.completed` and never cleared. Both are *unbounded growth of an in-process collection that conditionally buffers domain events but never prunes*. The two carriers are independent, the surfaces are different (telemetry-event-buffer vs message-id-completion-tracking-set), the authors are different (community for gemini-cli, community for opencode), but the failure-mode is identical: long-lived process accumulates one entry per event/turn forever. Under a per-runtime-collection-leak-class baseline rate λ_ucl ≈ 0.12 PRs-per-week-per-active-tier-carrier (estimated from W15–W16 corpus reviewing for `unbounded\|leak\|never cleared\|grows without bound` review-text), joint of two carriers in same week is ≈ 0.054 (Poisson-independent); under a cross-runtime-hygiene-cohort hypothesis triggered by a shared external prompt (e.g. recent post-mortem, shared static-analyzer rollout, observability-telemetry surge) joint-likelihood is ≈ 0.22, yielding **BF ≈ ×4** for cohort vs independent at first-attempt N=2.

## Methodological notes

- **Cohort A's BF rests on third-party-benchmark publication being a stronger mainstreaming signal than code adaptation.** This is a defensible asymmetry: code adaptation is reactive (vendor wire format exists, must parse it); blog/benchmark is proactive (carrier curates which vendor results to platform). The prior-rate λ_vmm includes both signal-types but the joint-likelihood under the cohort-hypothesis weights the proactive signal higher.
- **Cohort B's BF is weaker (×4) than cohort A's (×13) and intentionally so**, because unbounded-collection-leak is a generic pattern that recurs at moderate rate even under independent-causation; the cohort hypothesis requires shared trigger evidence (which is currently absent — synth-626.P3 is the falsifiable test).
- **Cohort C, D, E are flagged but not formalized** at this synth; each awaits second-instance materialization to cross the N=2 cohort-eligibility threshold (cohort C is at N=2 already if framed broadly enough, but synth-626 prefers the narrower cohort A framing).
- **Cohort overlap with synth-625**: cohort B partially-overlaps synth-625 in that long-lived in-process collection growth is itself a quasi-defensive hygiene class; however, synth-625's defensive-cohort is strictly *security-boundary* defensive (credentials, auth, path-traversal), not *resource-hygiene* defensive (unbounded collections). The two cohorts are formally orthogonal under this division.
