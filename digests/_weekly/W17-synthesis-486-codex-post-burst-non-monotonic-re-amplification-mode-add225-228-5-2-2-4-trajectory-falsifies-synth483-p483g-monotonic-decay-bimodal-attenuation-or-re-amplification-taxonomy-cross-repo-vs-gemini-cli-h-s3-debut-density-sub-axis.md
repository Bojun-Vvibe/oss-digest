## Synth #486 framework: bimodal attenuation-or-re-amplification taxonomy

We define two post-volume-event sub-modes for any repo emerging from a multi-PR burst or long-silence-chain-break:

- **Mode-A (Attenuation)**: post-event PR-cardinality follows monotonic non-increasing trajectory; queue-discharge per synth #480 sub-class B framework. Predicted modal trajectory: 5 → 2 → 1 → 0 with strict-decreasing shape; or 3 → 2 → 1 → 0; or 1 → 0 (single-tick spike per synth #93).
- **Mode-R (Re-amplification)**: post-event PR-cardinality exhibits non-monotonic trajectory with mid-tail rebound at +k tick (typically k ∈ {2, 3, 4}); reflects latent activity-pool re-engagement triggered by the initial burst's surface-class introduction. Predicted modal trajectory: 5 → 2 → 2 → 4 (Add.225-228 codex anchor); or 3 → 1 → 4 → 2 hypothetical analog.

The two modes are **mutually exclusive at the trajectory level** but probabilistically mixed at the prior level: any given post-event trajectory is generated under Mode-A with prior p_A and Mode-R with prior p_R = 1 − p_A.
