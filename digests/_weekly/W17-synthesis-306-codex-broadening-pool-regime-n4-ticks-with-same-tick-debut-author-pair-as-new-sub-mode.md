# W17 Synthesis #306 — Codex broadening-pool regime extends to n=4 consecutive ticks with same-tick debut-author pair as a new sub-mode, distinct from synth #285 multi-author disjoint sprint

**Thesis.** Across Add.134-Add.137 codex has surfaced a debut-class W17 author **every consecutive tick** (owenlin0 Add.134, gpeal Add.135, mzeng-openai Add.136, alexsong-oai+canvrno-oai Add.137) — a **debut-author-per-tick streak now at n=4 ticks**, the longest debut-streak observed in W17 codex. ADDENDUM-137 introduces a structurally novel sub-mode: **two debut-class authors merge in the same tick on disjoint subsystems** (alexsong-oai #19949 MCP-import vs canvrno-oai #19939 TUI-steer). This is distinct from synth #285's multi-sprint multi-author disjoint pattern, which required cross-tick anchoring and stable recurring authors. Synth #306 isolates **same-tick debut-pair** as the new sharp end of codex's broadening regime.

**Distinctness from neighbouring synths.**
- vs synth #285 (multi-sprint mode for carry-pred revival): synth #285 anchored on **recurring authors revived after multi-sprint silence**. Synth #306 anchors on **first-time-in-W17 authors arriving same-tick**. The two patterns are orthogonal on the prior-W17-presence axis: #285 requires prior presence + silence + revival; #306 requires absence-throughout-W17 + simultaneous arrival. Both can co-occur (and do, in Add.137: mzeng-openai/alexsong-oai/canvrno-oai are debuts while celia-oai is a recurring revival from her last W17 appearance), but they are distinct generative mechanisms.
- vs synth #302 (codex 4-author disjoint quadruplet batch as flush kinetic signature): synth #302's quadruplet was 4 distinct **recurring** authors with monotonically increasing spacing as a batch-flush. Add.137 has 4 distinct authors with **mixed debut/recurring composition** and **non-monotonic spacing** (00:45:24 → 00:51:31 → 01:09:20 → 01:10:40 = gaps 6m07s, 17m49s, 1m20s — not monotone, contains a sub-2-min terminal). Synth #306 differentiates by composition rather than kinetics.
- vs synth #303 candidate (Hona same-author back-to-back silence-break n=2 in opencode): synth #303 candidate's "first W17 same-author consecutive-tick presence" framing is **superseded** by bolinfest's n=3 consecutive-tick presence in codex (Add.135 #20010 → Add.136 #20041 → Add.137 #20108) — synth #306 records this supersession as a side-finding: codex's broadening-pool regime co-exists with a contracting-anchor regime (bolinfest as a fixed-point anchor across 3 ticks while debut authors rotate around him), forming a **anchor + rotating-debuts joint structure** unique to Add.135-137 codex.

**Falsifiability.**
- (a) If Add.138 codex emits zero debut-class W17 authors, the n=4 streak terminates at n=4 and synth #306's "regime" claim weakens to "4-tick anomaly" rather than a sustained mode.
- (b) If Add.138-141 (forward 4 ticks) codex sustains ≥1 debut author per tick, the broadening-pool extends to n=8 and the regime is established as a half-day-class W17 mode.
- (c) If a future codex tick contains ≥3 same-tick debuts, the same-tick-debut-pair sub-mode generalizes to a multiplet and synth #306 must be refined to handle higher-cardinality same-tick debut bursts.
- (d) If bolinfest does NOT appear in Add.138 codex, the anchor-side of the joint structure terminates at n=3 and the "anchor + rotating-debuts" framing reduces to a tick-local Add.135-137 phenomenon.
