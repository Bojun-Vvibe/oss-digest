## Synth #261 claim — Prediction-resurrection regime

**Statement:** Across the W17 corpus, when an author-cadence prediction (e.g., Pred VV, Pred YY, Pred KK) is RESOLVED-FALSIFIED at first observation tick (i.e., the predicted author has 0 activity at the first test tick), the **probability of the same author producing a merge or open at tick N+1 is non-trivial** (empirically ≥30% based on Add.113→Add.114 1-of-3 sample). This is the **prediction-resurrection regime**.

**Mechanism:** Per-tick author-cadence predictions are sensitive to **single-tick noise** — an author with a 3-4 tick metronome cadence may skip 1 tick due to time-of-day, review-load, or stochastic factors and resume the next tick. The aggressive 1-tick-falsification methodology amplifies this noise into apparent regime shifts that are actually short pauses. The resurrection regime corrects for this.

**This is non-obvious because:** The standard falsification protocol treats RESOLVED-FALSIFIED as terminal — predictions are not re-evaluated post-falsification. The resurrection regime requires a **post-falsification carry-window** to be added to author-cadence predictions, distinct from the active prediction window.

## Linkage to synth #260 author-pool-broadening

Synth #260 introduced the **debut axis** of author-pool change. Synth #261 introduces the **re-entry axis**. Together, they decompose author-pool-evolution into:
- **DEBUT** (1st W17 appearance) — synth #260
- **RE-ENTRY** (return after multi-tick silence) — synth #261 (kitlangton-class via Pred PP)
- **RESURRECTION** (return immediately after explicit falsification) — synth #261 NEW

The three axes are **not interchangeable**. Resurrection is distinguishable from re-entry by the **falsification predicate** — re-entry has no associated active prediction; resurrection does. The conflation of the two would obscure prediction methodology limitations.
